I am a second-year PhD student in Computer Science at New York University, advised by Hanan Salam at the SMART (Social Machines and Robotics) Lab. I am supported by the NYU Abu Dhabi Global PhD Fellowship. Before NYU I received my BS in Data Science from NYU Shanghai in 2023. I am also a member of the AAAC DEI Committee.

AI for everyone is AI for no one. I work on making large language models people-aware — attentive to an individual user’s preferences, affect, and interaction history, and able to adapt to them over time. I am drawn to this problem because generic alignment produces averaged behavior that fits no one in particular, and because personalization sits at the intersection of human cognition, social signals, and the practical limits of today’s foundation models.

My research combines multimodal user modeling, preference-aligned training, and agent memory to ask how LLM-based systems can evolve from tools into partners that understand you — moving AI roles from function execution to humanized companionship.

My research interests mainly lie in the following areas:

  • User-signal modeling & personalized interaction: building agents that deeply model multimodal user signals — preference, affect, personality — to produce coherent, per-user responses. See our work on preference adaptation in human–agent dialogue (PrefIx, ACL Findings 2026) and personality-aware engagement in online learning (MOPSE, ACM MM 2025 MRAC).

  • Preference alignment & data augmentation: scenario-driven innovation in instruction tuning, preference alignment, and data augmentation for under-represented users and per-user distributions (ACII 2025, Oral).

  • Long-term memory for LLM agents: frameworks that let an agent persistently perceive a user’s evolving preferences and context, and deliver highly customized responses across sessions (PRIME, under review).

  • Evaluation of human–AI collaboration: instruments for measuring when AI augments human creative or technical work, and when it instead flattens collective diversity — from text-to-image prompting (AtelierEval) to agentic coding (CentaurEval).

  • Affective & socially aware computing: foundations tying affective computing, social commonsense, and personality modeling into the design of persona-level agents (Survey on Personalized Affective Computing, arXiv 2023).
